About Least Authority Enterprises

Least Authority Enterprises was formed in 2011 to provide freedom-compatible storage. We are a small team who believe that freedom matters in storage technology. You can take advantage of online services while retaining control over your own data. We're here to make it easy.

Who Is Least Authority Enterprises?

Zooko Wilcox-O'Hearn
CEO / Engineering / Operations / Customer Support

Zooko Wilcox-O'Hearn has more than 15 years of experience in secure distributed systems, free/open source software, and startups. He is known for his work on DigiCash, Mojo Nation, ZRTP, and more. He is one of the co-founders of the Tahoe-LAFS project. He started Least Authority Enterprises because he wanted a convenient and safe way to store his files in the cloud.

David-Sarah Hopwood
Engineering / Operations / Customer Support

David-Sarah Hopwood participated in the standardization of the TLS protocol and Internationalized Domain Names, found security bugs and design flaws in Java Virtual Machines, wrote code for the Cryptix cryptography library, and did security auditing for the Caja Secure JavaScript project. They are a major contributor to the Tahoe-LAFS project. In their spare time, they are designing a capability-secure programming language called "Noether".

Zancas Wilcox
Engineering / Operations / Customer Support

Zancas is curious about how things work, so he has a problem with black boxes — he's constantly trying to open them up and see what's inside. He cares deeply about freedom and the continued advancement of human knowledge, and is excited to be part of Least Authority Enterprises.
